East African Cuisine

East African cuisine reflects the diversity of cultures in the region, including Swahili, Ethiopian, and Somali influences. Meals often feature ingredients such as maize, beans, cassava, and coconut. Popular cooking methods include boiling, steaming, and stewing with aromatic spices.

Signature Dishes

  • Injera (Ethiopia)
  • Isombe (Rwanda)
  • Nyama Choma (Kenya)

Common Ingredients

East African food typically includes cassava leaves, plantains, spices like cardamom and turmeric, and proteins such as goat meat and fish. Coconut milk is also widely used in coastal dishes.

Cooking Techniques

Many East African recipes use techniques like slow simmering, charcoal grilling, and fermentation—especially in dishes like Injera and fermented milk beverages.
