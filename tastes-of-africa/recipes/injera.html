Injera

Injera is a sourdough-risen flatbread with a slightly spongy texture, originating from Ethiopia and Eritrea. It's a staple food and is used both as a dish and as an edible utensil to scoop up stews and salads.

Cooking Instructions
  1. Mix teff flour with water and ferment for 2-3 days.
  2. Stir the batter and cook on a hot non-stick skillet.
  3. Cover for a minute until holes appear and it's cooked through.
  4. Serve warm with Ethiopian stews like Doro Wat.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups teff flour
  • 3 cups water
  • Salt to taste
